P - Oneheart priest - (a phylactery of fortitude)
D - wizened old man (a phylactery of free movement, a thornspike staff - wielded)

Aggressive purebloods at the swamp. Using spears and impaling.
 A manor infested with rats beyond the swamp?

Paths to different NPCs seem easiest found by room landmarks, but there is no sense of
direction given, so finding the path is a matter of trial and error. This is really easy
with a familiar. Not so easy without one.

There are a few set paths, however, and all use the first step into the 
swamp on the east side as the starting point.

Path to Chosen One Healer - swwwnnn
1. golden web, glittering in the light (this is the entrance to the swamp) - go south one
2. A water hickory grows here - w
3. muskrat lodge - w
4. explain these scorch-marks... - w
5. The water is still here - n
6. No wonder, then, that the whole swamp seems alive - n
7. golden mouse sitting very quietly on a tree trunk - n to the healer

Path to Jara Silverstaff - swssswsws
(Move to each of these landmarks consecutively, then south, west, south.)
1. golden web, glittering in the light (this is the entrance to the swamp) - s
2. A water hickory grows here - w
3. muskrat lodge - s
4. a beautiful copper-winged butterfly - s
5. brightly striped snake - s
6. A gray owl - w
7. You could easily climb the southern rise - s
8. w
9. s

Path to Shaman (probably. this is also the easiest to stumble upon) (from one south of healer it can be (maybe) ew then nn to shaman)
1. golden web, glittering in the light (this is the entrance to the swamp)
2. easier to get lost
3. and... and... and...
4. you discover a clump of saltweed
5. an insidious fungus
6. north from the above room

Path to Druid (in progress)
1. wander the lavendar until you look all and see: "The swamp gets shallower, but is filled with rushes and cattails."s
2. could be a leftover from the effects of the flowers - look all shows "The swamp gets shallower this way." - e
3. aggro razor weed is the next room - e
4. s to get attacked by the razorweed, then s to the strangleweed.
5. e to get attacked by the strangleweed, then e to the druid.
